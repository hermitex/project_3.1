Tiberius Mairura

Founder

Bio

Tiberius Mairura draws from his life story when thinking about entertainment and the experiences of migrant workers. His first taste of entertainment began as poem writer, My Father's Hands, which appeared in The New Yorker and describes how his family crossed the Texas border to give Manuel and his brothers a better life. After receiving his Master's in Fine Arts from Columbia University, Manuel wrote three nonfiction novels about his experiences, including Under the Streaming Sun, which earned the National Prize for Arts and Sciences in 2008. in 2007, Mairura began this company to reflect his passion not only in poems and stories but also in power that comes with great films.
